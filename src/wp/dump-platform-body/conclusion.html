Conclusion

When you're choosing between a dump body and a platform body for your Ford work truck, especially here in the Pacific Northwest, it really comes down to what you're hauling every day.

Dump bodies are the go-to choice if you're moving a lot of bulk materials, dirt, gravel, debris, or anything you need to unload quickly. Construction crews and waste management companies rely on them because the hydraulic lift does the heavy lifting for you. The downside? Those hydraulic systems need regular maintenance to keep working smoothly.

Ford's trucks, from the F-350 up to the F-750, work well with either setup. You've got gas and diesel engine options, plus that 10-speed transmission that handles heavy loads without breaking a sweat. The F-600 is interesting because it gives you more capability than you'd expect from its size class.

Working in our region adds a few wrinkles to consider. All that rain means dealing with wet, heavy materials that stick to everything, so dump bodies need to handle more challenging unloads. In Seattle, you're also dealing with tight streets and parking, so truck size matters more than it might elsewhere.
