Interior and Exterior Design, Space, and Practicality


A compact SUV must be a master of practicality, blending stylish design with a versatile and spacious interior. Both the Kona and Crosstrek are five-seaters, but their design choices reveal different priorities that directly impact their daily usability.


Exterior Styling: Future Forward vs. Rugged Utilitarian

The 2026 Hyundai Kona makes a bold design statement. Its futuristic aesthetic is defined by a clean, sculpted body and the distinctive Seamless Horizon Lamp that stretches across the front fascia, creating an unmistakable and modern presence. It's a design that looks perfectly at home in the vibrant, evolving urban landscape of Oklahoma City.


The 2026 Subaru Crosstrek, by contrast, employs a more evolutionary and utilitarian design language. Its look is rugged and functional, with aggressive body cladding and a familiar silhouette that clearly communicates its off-road intentions. While handsome, it is a more conservative approach that prioritizes function over form.


Interior Experience and Utility

Stepping inside the Kona reveals a driver-centric cockpit that feels a class above. The undisputed centerpiece is the panoramic display that houses a stunning 12.3-inch touchscreen, which is standard on every single trim level. This immediately establishes a premium, high-tech atmosphere, even for the most budget-conscious buyer.

The Crosstrek's interior is well-built and functional, but more traditional in its execution. While it offers a large 11.6-inch screen on higher trims, base models make do with a smaller, less immersive setup.


When it comes to practicality, the dimensions tell a crucial story. The Crosstrek boasts superior ground clearance, with 8.7 inches on standard models and an impressive 9.3 inches on the Wilderness trim. This is a clear advantage for serious off-roading. However, this focus on clearance comes with a trade-off in interior volume. The 2026 Kona offers a significantly larger maximum cargo capacity, boasting up to 63.7 cubic feet with the rear seats folded down. The Crosstrek trails considerably, with figures ranging from 50.6 to 55.3 cubic feet.


For the typical Oklahoma family, this difference is profound. The Kona's superior cargo space is the more valuable asset for 99% of driving situations. The Kona is designed from the inside out, prioritizing the space that people and their belongings occupy in the real world.
