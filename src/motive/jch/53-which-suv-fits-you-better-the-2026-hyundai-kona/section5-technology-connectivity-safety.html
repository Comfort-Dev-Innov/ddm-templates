2026 Kona vs 2026 Crosstrek: Technology, Connectivity, and Safety


In today's connected world, a vehicle's technology suite is as important as its engine. This is where the 2026 Hyundai Kona establishes its most dominant advantage, offering a level of standard technology and available features that redefine value in the compact SUV class.


Infotainment Supremacy

Hyundai has democratized technology in the 2026 Kona. Every model, from the entry-level SE to the fully-loaded Limited, comes standard with a brilliant 12.3-inch high-resolution touchscreen featuring wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. This single feature transforms the daily driving experience, providing a seamless, intuitive, and visually stunning interface for navigation, communication, and entertainment.


The Subaru Crosstrek reserves its best technology for those who pay more. Base models are equipped with a smaller 7-inch or dual 7-inch screen setup, with the larger 11.6-inch screen only becoming available on higher trims. A Kona buyer on a budget enjoys a premium digital experience, while a Crosstrek buyer at a similar price point is met with a cabin that looks and feels less advanced.


This pattern continues across the feature list. The Kona Limited offers near-luxury amenities like a Bose® Premium Audio system, ventilated front seats, a hands-free smart liftgate, and Hyundai Digital Key 2 Touch, which allows your smartphone to function as your key. These features showcase the Kona's ability to provide a truly premium experience at a mainstream price point.


Safety Suites

Both Hyundai and Subaru are renowned for their commitment to safety, and both vehicles come equipped with excellent suites of driver-assistance technologies. The Crosstrek features its standard EyeSight® Driver Assist Technology, while the Kona comes with Hyundai SmartSense. Both systems include crucial features like:


  • Forward Collision-Avoidance Assist / Pre-Collision Braking
  • Lane Keeping Assist
  • Smart / Adaptive Cruise Control

Both suites provide an essential layer of protection and confidence. However, the Kona again pushes the technological envelope on its higher trims. The Kona Limited offers a Blind-Spot View Monitor, which displays a live video feed of your blind spot in the digital instrument cluster when you activate your turn signal, and a Surround View Monitor, which provides a 360-degree bird's-eye view around the vehicle for effortless maneuvering in tight spaces. These advanced visual aids offer a level of situational awareness that is invaluable in crowded parking lots and complex urban environments.
