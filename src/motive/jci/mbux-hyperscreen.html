Smarter Than It Looks: The "Zero Layer" Interface

A screen this size could easily become cluttered, but Mercedes-Benz solved this with the "Zero Layer" concept. Using Artificial Intelligence, the system learns your habits and places the most important apps directly on the home screen.

  • No Digging for Menus: If you always call your spouse on the drive home at 5:30 PM, the phone tile automatically appears at that time.
  • Context Aware: If you approach a steep driveway where you usually raise the suspension, the "Raise Vehicle" button will pop up automatically.
  • Always-On Map: The navigation map remains the background at all times, so you never lose your sense of direction while changing music or adjusting the climate.

Safety First: The Invisible Digital Shield

One common question we hear at Jackie Cooper Mercedes-Benz is, "Does the passenger screen distract the driver?" Mercedes-Benz engineers have created a clever solution:

  • Active Dimming Technology: The vehicle uses eye-tracking cameras to monitor the driver. If the driver looks at the passenger screen while the car is in motion, the system immediately dims that specific section of the glass to black so the driver cannot see the content (like a movie), while the passenger continues watching uninterrupted.
  • Digital Blocking: Your passenger gets a dedicated entertainment zone, and you get zero distractions—keeping your attention locked on the drive.

Dimensions and Design: How Big Is the Mercedes EQS Hyperscreen?

One of the most striking features of the MBUX Hyperscreen is its impressive size and integrated design. The Hyperscreen in the Mercedes EQS Sedan spans a massive 56 inches diagonally across the dashboard, creating an immersive digital landscape for occupants.

This expansive panel includes:

Living with Glass: Care and Maintenance

The Hyperscreen is a single piece of curved Gorilla Glass spanning 56 inches. Keeping it pristine is simple if you follow these tips:

  • Microfiber Only: Always use a clean, dry microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints.
  • Avoid Ammonia: Never use standard window cleaners (like Windex) or paper towels, which can damage the oleophobic (fingerprint-resistant) coating.
  • Screen Clean Mode: In the settings menu, you can activate "Cleaning Mode," which temporarily locks the touchscreen so you can wipe it down without accidentally changing your radio station or AC settings.
