When you see "Phone Calls" in your Google Ads dashboard, you probably think it means "people who called my business and spoke to someone."

That's not what it means.

According to Google's own definition: "Phone calls are the number of times people clicked on a phone number displayed in an ad on a mobile device or manually dialed the Google forwarding number."

Read that carefully. It's the number of times someone clicked or dialed. Not the number of conversations. Not even the number of times your phone rang.

Just clicks and dials.

Here's what we discovered:

For this business, we installed CallRail, a third-party call tracking system. We set up a local tracking number and made sure it was properly connected to all the ad assets. Then we listened to every single call that came through.

Google's number was just off by nearly 10x.

Understanding "Call from Ads" Conversions

Now, you might be thinking: "But I don't use the Phone Calls metric. I use 'Call from Ads' conversions, which require the call to last longer than 30 seconds."

That's better. But it's still not telling you what you think it's telling you.

Why This Happens (And Why It Matters)

We don't entirely understand why this discrepancy occurs at this scale, and it may not affect every account. Your phone call metrics might be more accurate.

But here's what we know for certain: this happens. We see it, we've documented it. And if you're not verifying your phone call data with an independent tracking system, you have no way of knowing if it's happening to you.
