Interior Dimensions, Comfort, and Material Quality

For families spending hours on the road between Bellevue, Kent, and beyond, cabin comfort and space are paramount. The Explorer capitalizes on a reimagined interior featuring upgraded soft-touch surfaces, intricate textures, and thoughtful color accents that elevate the environment well above class norms. Though the third-row armrests remain a hard plastic in all models, the availability of power-folding seats on select trims greatly enhances usability.

The Explorer offers generous front-row space, superior middle-row comfort with captain's chair options (standard on ST), and ample cargo management options. Its multi-textured interior with ambient lighting options fosters an upscale ambiance compared to the Pilot's more utilitarian layout and materials.

Honda's Pilot does provide competent space with flexible seating arrangements, including a stowable second-row center seat for an eight-passenger capacity, but many materials feel more conventional and less impressively refined than those in the refreshed Explorer cabin.

Seating and Convenience

Explorer's tri-zone climate controls, power-adjustable heated front seats, and available ventilated seating on higher trims elevate passenger comfort for long drives through Lake Sammamish or Mount Rainier trips. The Pilot offers similar features at upper trims, but lacks the breadth of ambient lighting and interior customization offered by Ford.
