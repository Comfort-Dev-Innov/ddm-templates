Range Performance & Charging

Efficiency, Range, and Charging Insights

How the Mustang Mach-E and Tesla Model Y compare in real-world range, charging capabilities, and energy efficiency for Kent-area drivers.

Mach-E Efficiency

Competitive Range & Smart Charging

While Tesla is known for remarkable range capabilities, the Mach-E provides highly competitive mileage that balances driving range with energy efficiency and usability in typical Northwest conditions. The Mach-E's extended battery RWD model achieves up to approximately 320 miles EPA range, with AWD versions covering ample distances between charges.

Charging speeds are robust, with extended-range battery variants benefiting from up to 150 kW DC fast charging, promoting rapid top-offs in under 40 minutes when using public networks like the growing Electrify America infrastructure. The Mustang includes a standard heat pump system, enhancing cold-weather range retention, ideal for Puget Sound's varying climate.

Tesla Performance

Extended Range with Trade-offs

Tesla's Model Y Long Range AWD eclipses with up to 337 miles EPA-estimated range and benefits from Tesla's up to 250 kW Supercharging network, ensuring surety in long-distance travel. However, real-world efficiency fluctuates more widely, and 2025 Model Y customers must anticipate deferred ride quality improvements until the 2026 redesign.
