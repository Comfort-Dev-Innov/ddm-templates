Fuel Efficiency and Economy Breakdown

Though fuel economy remains a challenge in this segment, the Expedition’s twin-turbo V6 engines deliver notably better mileage than the Suburban’s traditional V8s. The SUV achieves an EPA estimated 17 mpg city and 23 mpg highway in rear-wheel drive, versus approximately 15 mpg city and 20 mpg highway for the Suburban’s gasoline V8. The diesel variant of the Suburban does yield improved efficiency, but at the cost of slower acceleration and limited availability.

Furthermore, the Expedition’s extensive range of approximately 630 miles per fill provides fewer stops on long trips around Western Washington, which can be critical for families traveling from Covington to Seattle.
