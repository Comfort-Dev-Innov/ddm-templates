Standard Modes for Your Everyday Washington Drive

Most 2025 Ford Bronco models come equipped with a set of five standard G.O.A.T. Modes designed to handle the day-to-day scenarios every Kent driver encounters.

Normal:

This is your go-to setting for everyday driving. Perfect for running errands on Kent-Kangley Road or cruising down Central Avenue, Normal mode provides a balanced blend of performance, comfort, and fuel efficiency.

Eco:

Stuck in traffic on I-5? Switch to Eco mode. This setting is designed to maximize fuel economy by softening the throttle response and optimizing transmission shifts for efficiency, helping you save at the pump during your commute.

Sport:

When the road opens up, Sport mode injects a dose of excitement into your drive. It sharpens throttle response, adjusts shift patterns for a more aggressive feel, and tightens steering for enhanced feedback. It's the perfect way to enjoy a spirited drive on a scenic route like Green Valley Road.

Slippery:

A non-negotiable for anyone living in the Puget Sound region. During our infamous rainy seasons, Slippery mode provides extra confidence on wet or icy pavement. It lowers throttle response and adjusts the traction and stability control systems to help prevent wheelspin and maintain grip.

Sand:

While you might not find dunes in downtown Kent, this mode is perfect for adventures to the Washington coast or navigating loose gravel access roads. It optimizes power delivery and transmission shifts to maintain momentum and prevent the Bronco from getting bogged down in soft terrain.

Advanced Off-Road Modes: Unleash Your Wild Side

For those who hear the call of the mountains, higher-level Bronco trims, such as the Badlands, Wildtrak, and Raptor, offer specialized modes to conquer the most demanding trails. These are the modes that truly let you explore the backcountry around areas like Mount Rainier National Park or the Evans Creek ORV Park.

Mud/Ruts:

After a classic Washington downpour, local trails can become a muddy challenge. Available on trims like the Black Diamond and Badlands, this mode engages the 4x4 High system and fine-tunes the traction control to allow for some wheelspin, which helps clear mud from the tire treads and pull you through the muck.

Rock Crawl:

Found on the most capable off-road trims, Rock Crawl is for serious, low-speed technical challenges. It engages 4x4 Low, locks the rear differential (if equipped), and provides maximum throttle control for precisely navigating large obstacles on trails in the Mount Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest.

Baja (Raptor):

This mode is engineered for high-speed, off-road performance. While named for desert racing, it’s perfectly suited for flying down gravel forest service roads in the Cascades. Baja mode optimizes suspension, reduces turbo lag, and holds gears longer for maximum power and control when the trail opens up.

Tow-Haul (Raptor):

Need to pull your boat to Lake Tapps or haul a trailer for a camping trip? The Bronco Raptor’s Tow-Haul mode adjusts transmission shift points to keep the engine in its powerband, making towing heavy loads feel effortless and secure.
