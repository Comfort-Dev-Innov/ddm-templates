The Heart of the Matter: Performance and Capability

The most significant distinction between the 2025 Ford Bronco and the Bronco Sport lies under the hood and in their foundational design. The 2025 Ford Bronco is a body-on-frame SUV, sharing its tough underpinnings with the Ford Ranger. This construction is ideal for serious off-roading and provides a commanding presence on the road. Kent drivers can choose between two potent engine options:

  • A standard 2.3-liter EcoBoost® inline-four engine, delivering a robust 300 horsepower and 325 lb.-ft. of torque.
  • An available 2.7-liter EcoBoost® V6 engine, churning out an impressive 330 horsepower and 415 lb.-ft. of torque for those who demand maximum power.

The Bronco comes standard with a 4x4 drivetrain and offers either a 7-speed manual transmission (a rarity in today's market) or a smooth-shifting 10-speed automatic.

In contrast, the 2025 Ford Bronco Sport is a unibody crossover based on the Ford Escape platform. This design offers a more comfortable and car-like ride, ideal for navigating Kent's city streets and highways with improved fuel efficiency. The Bronco Sport still packs a punch with its standard 4x4 capability and two available EcoBoost® engines:

  • A 1.5-liter EcoBoost® three-cylinder engine with 181 horsepower and 190 lb.-ft. of torque, offering an excellent blend of power and efficiency for daily driving.
  • A more powerful 2.0-liter EcoBoost four-cylinder engine is available on higher trims, delivering a spirited 250 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque. of torque.

Both Bronco Sport engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

Ready for Adventure: Off-Road Prowess

For the true off-road enthusiast in the Kent area, the 2025 Ford Bronco is the undisputed champion. Its robust construction, higher ground clearance, and advanced off-road technology, including the Terrain Management System™ with G.O.A.T. Modes™ (Goes Over Any Type of Terrain), make it capable of tackling the most challenging trails near Mount Rainier or the Snoqualmie National Forest. With available features like front and rear locking differentials, a disconnecting front stabilizer bar, and massive 35-inch all-terrain tires, the Bronco is built to conquer the wild.

The 2025 Ford Bronco Sport, while not as extreme as its larger counterpart, is no slouch when the pavement ends. Its standard 4x4 system and G.O.A.T. Modes™ provide confident handling on slippery roads and light trails. For those seeking more aggressive off-road capability, the Badlands™ trim offers an advanced 4x4 system with a twin-clutch rear-drive unit, all-terrain tires, and an off-road-tuned suspension. This makes the Bronco Sport a fantastic choice for weekend camping trips, visits to Lake Tapps, or exploring the scenic backroads of the Pacific Northwest.

Creature Comforts and Technology

Inside, both Broncos offer a blend of rugged functionality and modern technology. The 2025 Ford Bronco boasts a purpose-built interior crafted from durable materials, complemented by available washout floors with integrated drains, making it effortless to clean up after a muddy adventure. The centerpiece of the dashboard is a large touchscreen featuring Ford's SYNC 4 infotainment system, which offers seamless smartphone integration and navigation. The Bronco's iconic removable doors and top provide an unparalleled open-air experience.

The 2025 Ford Bronco Sport boasts a comfortable and spacious interior with clever storage solutions, including an available under-seat storage compartment in the second row. The user-friendly SYNC® 4 system is also standard, displayed on a generous touchscreen. The Bronco Sport is packed with standard and available Ford Co-Pilot360™ driver-assist technologies, providing peace of mind on your daily commute through the Kent Valley.
