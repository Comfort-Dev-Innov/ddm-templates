Introducing the 2026 Nissan Murano Base

Designed for families and commuters alike, the 2026 Murano Base blends Nissan’s signature engineering with practical luxury for a ride that is as reliable as it is comfortable. This midsize SUV offers seating for five and generous space for both passengers and cargo, making it ideally suited to meet the diverse needs of drivers from Broken Arrow to Tulsa.

This generation of Murano continues the fourth-generation design evolution first introduced in 2025, emphasizing a modern aesthetic and elevated driving experience. The Base trim anchors the lineup with a powerful yet efficient 2.0-liter Variable Compression Turbocharged engine producing 241 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. It pairs with a 9-speed automatic transmission designed to deliver smooth gear changes and a confident feel, whether navigating city streets or open highways around Tulsa.

Fuel efficiency plays an important role here, with EPA ratings reaching up to 21 mpg city and 27 mpg highway—numbers that align well with everyday driving patterns typical in the Tulsa metropolitan area. The standard front-wheel drive on the Base keeps the vehicle accessible and straightforward, while all-wheel-drive options are available for those requiring added traction in varying weather.

Performance and Driving Experience on Tulsa Roads

The Murano Base's heart is its 2.0L VC-Turbo four-cylinder engine, a refined powertrain that balances spirited performance with everyday efficiency. Delivering 241 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, this engine springs to life, providing smooth acceleration, with independent tests measuring around 7.2 seconds from 0 to 60 mph.

Driving through Tulsa’s varying conditions, the nine-speed automatic transmission complements the engine smoothly, providing responsive shifts without the lags sometimes associated with turbocharged variable compression technology. While the chassis design carries some legacy elements, steering remains precise, and suspension tuning delivers an adequately comfortable ride across different road surfaces.

The Base’s front-wheel-drive configuration offers confident traction in dry to moderately wet conditions typically found in the Tulsa area. For those seeking traction control for more challenging conditions, all-wheel drive is available on higher trims, adding peace of mind for winter weather or off-pavement excursions near Broken Arrow.

Safety and Driver Assistance Systems
