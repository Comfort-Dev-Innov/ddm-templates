Introducing the 2025 Nissan Pathfinder Base

Open the next chapter of your family's journey with the 2025 Nissan Pathfinder Base, a three-row SUV crafted for comfort on the roads of Tulsa, Broken Arrow, and beyond. Launched originally in 2022, this generation continues to impress with a well-rounded blend of space, family-oriented features, and trusted reliability.

Built to seat up to eight passengers, the Pathfinder Base creates a versatile environment for everyday commutes and weekend adventures. This model caters to those who value ample seating, ease of access, and a confident driving experience without the complexity or heft of overly specialized features. With a Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price (MSRP) starting near $36,400, it remains an accessible option for families prioritizing quality and longevity.

The vehicle’s front-engine layout complements available front-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations, allowing drivers to tailor traction and handling to Oklahoma’s varied road conditions—from urban streets in Tulsa to more challenging surfaces near Broken Arrow.

Balancing Family Needs and Practicality

The Nissan Pathfinder Base targets families who need three-row seating while maintaining a manageable size for city driving. Spacious but not oversized, it offers thoughtful door design, with wide-opening rear doors that enhance entry and exit comfort, especially for accessing the third row, where legroom measures a respectful 28.0 inches.

Though competitors like the Toyota Grand Highlander and Subaru Ascent offer slightly more passenger and cargo room, the Pathfinder places a premium on user-friendly features and dependable performance, making it a dependable companion for area families. The inclusion of the Nissan Maintenance Care program for 2025 models adds peace of mind, with free oil changes scheduled for the first 24,000 miles or 2 years.

Exterior Styling: Practical Meets Rugged

Though the Pathfinder Base opts for a clean, understated design, it retains purposeful styling elements that illustrate Nissan’s balance of modern utility and confident presence.

Simplicity with Function in Mind

The exterior dimensions reflect a well-proportioned midsize SUV, measuring 197.7 inches in length and 114.2 inches in wheelbase. Its nearly 78-inch width supports stable handling without compromising maneuverability in Tulsa’s neighborhoods. A wide track and sturdy stance are complemented by available alloy wheels designed to handle both everyday commutes and family road trips. While the Base trim forgoes the rugged accents of Rock Creek models, it maintains a durable presence, including integrated roof rails for added cargo-carrying versatility.

Features That Make Life Easier

The rear cargo area features a generous opening and efficient load floor, allowing family gear or groceries to be stored with less hassle. Though the power-operated rear liftgate isn’t standard on the Base trim, it's available on higher trims, offering convenience for larger cargo items. Fuel tank placement and aerodynamic design help achieve respectable fuel economy, solidifying its status as a family-practicality-focused SUV.

Performance and Driving Dynamics Explored

Under the hood, the 2025 Nissan Pathfinder Base is powered by a naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine generating 284 horsepower and 259 lb-ft of torque. This powertrain couples with a refined nine-speed automatic transmission to deliver a smooth, predictable ride that suits everyday driving in Oklahoma’s urban and rural settings.

Engine and Transmission Details

The V6 engine is designed to prioritize strong top-end power, with peak output achieved at 6,400 rpm. While it is effective for highway passing and steady cruising, low-end torque is moderate, which may affect initial acceleration feel, especially when fully loaded or towing.

Front-wheel drive comes standard, with all-wheel drive available to improve traction in wet or challenging conditions frequently experienced during Oklahoma winters or off-pavement excursions near Broken Arrow. Acceleration from zero to 60 mph is accomplished in approximately 7.1 seconds with FWD and slightly faster with AWD.

Refined Ride with Room for Growth

The Pathfinder’s handling leans toward comfort with a generally smooth suspension setup that absorbs road irregularities well. However, the steering is somewhat slower to respond than that of more dynamic midsize SUVs, which, along with a relatively large turning radius, may require some adjustment when maneuvering in tight spaces.

While off-road capability is limited, the Rock Creek trim notably improves ground clearance and features specialized tires for light rugged terrain. The Base trim, however, focuses on reliable on-road performance, making it a pragmatic choice for families mostly navigating paved roads.

Interior Space and Comfort for Family Life

Inside, the Pathfinder Base is designed to accommodate families with a focus on accessibility, flexibility, and moderate comfort. It provides three rows of seating for 7 to 8 passengers, depending on the configuration, making it a favorite among Tulsa families seeking versatility without sacrificing space.

Ample Seating and Thoughtful Layout

The front row offers 44.3 inches of legroom, while second-row passengers enjoy 35.5 inches. The third row, while not the most spacious in the segment, measures 28.0 inches, sufficient for children or occasional adult use. Rear doors open wide to help passengers, including families with child seats, move in and out with ease.

Flexible Cargo Areas

Cargo space behind the third row accommodates 16.6 cubic feet of luggage, increasing to 45.0 cubic feet with the third row folded, and reaching a maximum of 80.5 cubic feet when both the second and third rows are folded flat. While these volumes trail slightly behind segment leaders, they remain practical for everyday family needs such as groceries, sports gear, or weekend camping supplies.

Throughout the cabin, Nissan includes well-placed storage compartments and cupholders to keep essentials organized and accessible during travel. Materials in the Base trim are functional and durable, meeting expectations for the segment with a sharp priority on reliability over luxury.

Safety Features and Driver Assist Systems

Safety remains a cornerstone of the 2025 Nissan Pathfinder’s appeal, reflecting Nissan’s longstanding commitment to community protection with advanced technologies tailored for families around Broken Arrow and Tulsa.

Comprehensive Standard Safety Suite

The Base trim comes equipped with robust active safety systems, including:

  • Automatic emergency braking with pedestrian detection, promoting collision avoidance in city and highway traffic
  • Blind-spot monitoring and rear cross-traffic alert to enhance situational awareness
  • Lane-keep assist supporting safer lane management on highways and busy roads
  • Automatic high-beam control adjusts lighting intensity based on traffic conditions

Available Advanced Assistance Technologies

On higher trims such as SV, SL, and Platinum, Nissan’s ProPILOT Assist system introduces semi-autonomous driving features, including adaptive cruise control and lane-centering support—perfect for long Oklahoma drives or heavy traffic.

Nissan’s safety engineering is recognized by industry authorities: the 2025 Pathfinder received a five-star overall rating from NHTSA and continues the IIHS TOP SAFETY PICK+ legacy for models built after late 2023. These distinctions affirm the SUV’s ability to protect families with well-integrated passive and active safety measures.

Trim Levels and Available Packages

The 2025 Nissan Pathfinder is available in multiple configurations, allowing buyers to balance budget and feature choices based on their lifestyle. The Base trim represents the entry-level offering, presenting key essentials with the ability to upgrade as needs evolve.

Base Trim: Essentials for Everyday Families

As the foundation of the lineup, the Base model includes:

  • 3.5-liter V6 engine with 284 hp
  • Nine-speed automatic transmission
  • Front-wheel drive standard, AWD option available
  • Three-row seating for up to eight occupants
  • Standard safety and infotainment features detailed above
  • Capacity to tow up to 3,500 pounds

Beyond the Base: What Comes Next

Moving up to SV, SL, Rock Creek, and Platinum trims unlocks:

  • Advanced audio and connectivity options, including larger touchscreens and Bose premium sound
  • ProPILOT Assist semi-autonomous features starting at SV trim
  • Rock Creek trim with off-road styling, lifted suspension, and all-terrain tires for limited rugged driving
  • Increased towing capacity up to 6,000 pounds on select trims and packages
  • Premium interior materials, panoramic sunroof, and heated/ventilated seating

This flexibility allows Tulsa buyers to tailor the Pathfinder to their preferred balance of comfort, utility, and adventure readiness.
