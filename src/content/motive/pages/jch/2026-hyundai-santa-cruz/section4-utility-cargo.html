Utility and Cargo Capacity: Hyundai Santa Cruz vs Nissan Frontier

How these two vehicles define "utility" is a study in contrast, with the Santa Cruz focusing on innovative, lifestyle-oriented features and the Frontier prioritizing raw capacity and traditional flexibility.

The 2026 Santa Cruz features a highly versatile 4.3-foot composite bed that is packed with clever solutions for daily use. Its standout feature is a lockable, waterproof under-floor storage compartment, which functions like a car's trunk and even includes a drain plug, making it perfect for use as a cooler. This provides secure, weatherproof storage for groceries, tools, or gear without the need for a separate toolbox or tonneau cover. Other smart touches include integrated corner bumper steps for easy bed access and an available factory-integrated retractable tonneau cover. The Santa Cruz has a maximum payload capacity of 1,411 pounds and can tow up to 3,500 pounds with the base engine or a substantial 5,000 pounds with the turbocharged engine and AWD.

The 2026 Frontier is all about traditional truck capability and scale. It offers buyers a choice between a standard 5-foot bed or a longer 6-foot bed, a key advantage for those hauling lumber, motorcycles, or other lengthy items. Its utility is enhanced by the available Utili-track® Channel System, a highly regarded feature with three rails and adjustable cleats for securing cargo of various shapes and sizes. When it comes to raw power, the Frontier is the clear leader, boasting a maximum towing capacity of up to 7,150 pounds and a payload capacity of up to 1,620 pounds. This makes it the undisputed choice for owners who need to tow larger boats, campers, or heavy equipment trailers.

The choice comes down to the nature of the load. For lifestyle gear, home improvement supplies, and everyday items, the Santa Cruz's secure, convenient, and innovative features are arguably more practical. For serious work, construction materials, or large recreational toys, the Frontier's superior raw capacity is non-negotiable.
