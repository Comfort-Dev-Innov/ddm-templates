Power and Performance on Oklahoma's Roads

The philosophical differences between the IONIQ 9 and Santa Cruz are most apparent in their powertrains. One offers the silent, instantaneous rush of electric torque, while the other provides the familiar, responsive feel of refined gasoline power. A surprising point of parity is their maximum towing capacity, which levels the playing field on a key utility metric and shifts the decision toward the character of the performance one desires.

IONIQ 9

The IONIQ 9's all-electric powertrain portfolio is engineered to meet a wide spectrum of driving preferences. The lineup includes a standard-range Rear-Wheel-Drive (RWD) model producing 215 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, optimized for maximum efficiency. For those needing all-weather capability, a Long-Range All-Wheel-Drive (AWD) version offers a robust 303 horsepower and 446 lb-ft of torque.At the top of the range, the Performance AWD models unleash a staggering 422 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, capable of accelerating the three-row SUV from 0 to 60 mph in as little as 4.4 secondsâ€”quicker than many dedicated sports cars.

Underpinning this performance is a massive 110.3 kWh battery pack and a state-of-the-art 800-volt electrical architecture. This advanced system enables ultra-fast DC charging, replenishing the battery from 10% to 80% in approximately 24 minutes under ideal conditions. For an Oklahoma family, this translates to remarkable convenience on road trips. A journey from Midwest City to Dallas would require only a single, brief 25-minute stop. Furthermore, the inclusion of a native NACS (Tesla) charging port provides access to the vast and reliable Supercharger network, a significant advantage for travel across Oklahoma and beyond.

Santa Cruz

The Santa Cruz offers two proven and efficient gasoline engine options. The standard 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces 191 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, providing a reliable and economical choice for daily driving and commuting. For those who require more muscle for hauling or spirited driving, an available 2.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers a potent 281 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque.

A significant update for the 2026 model year is the move from a dual-clutch transmission (DCT) to a conventional 8-speed automatic on turbocharged models. This strategic shift prioritizes smooth, refined operation, particularly in city traffic, and enhances the perception of long-term reliability for a broader range of buyers. The Santa Cruz is available with standard Front-Wheel Drive (FWD) for maximum efficiency or with Hyundai's capable HTRAC All-Wheel Drive (AWD), which provides superior traction and confidence for navigating Oklahoma's unpredictable weather, from sudden summer downpours to the occasional winter ice storm.
