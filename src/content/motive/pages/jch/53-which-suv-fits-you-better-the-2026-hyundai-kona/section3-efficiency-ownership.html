Efficiency and Ownership of The 2026 Kona and 2026 Crosstrek


The initial purchase price is only the beginning of a vehicle's story. True value is revealed over years of ownership, measured in fuel costs and the peace of mind that comes from long-term reliability. In these crucial areas, the 2026 Hyundai Kona builds an insurmountable lead.


Fuel Economy Breakdown

For drivers looking to maximize their fuel budget, the Kona's flexible drivetrain options pay significant dividends. The standard 2.0L engine with FWD achieves an excellent EPA-estimated 29 MPG in the city and 34 MPG on the highway. Even the powerful 1.6L Turbo with FWD remains efficient at 26 MPG city and 31 MPG highway.


The 2026 Crosstrek, with its mandatory AWD system, offers respectable but lower figures for its gasoline model, estimated at around 27 MPG city and 33 MPG highway. The new Crosstrek Hybrid makes a significant leap, with an EPA-estimated 36 MPG combined. While impressive, this efficiency comes with a starting MSRP that is thousands of dollars higher than even the top-tier gasoline-powered Kona Limited, requiring many years of driving to recoup the initial investment through fuel savings.

The Ultimate Peace of Mind: America's Best Warranty

The single most compelling aspect of long-term ownership is Hyundai's industry-leading warranty, a testament to the company's confidence in its engineering and build quality. Every 2026 Hyundai Kona is protected by:

  • A 5-year/60,000-mile New Vehicle Limited Warranty.
  • A 10-year/100,000-mile Powertrain Limited Warranty.

This stands in stark contrast to the coverage offered on the 2026 Subaru Crosstrek:

  • A 3-year/36,000-mile New Vehicle Limited Warranty.
  • A 5-year/60,000-mile Powertrain Limited Warranty.

The difference is not trivial. The Kona's basic warranty provides two additional years and 24,000 additional miles of comprehensive protection. This means that in the fourth and fifth years of ownership, a potentially costly repair to the infotainment system, climate control, or other advanced electronics would be fully covered for the Kona owner, while the Crosstrek owner would face an out-of-pocket expense. This extended coverage represents thousands of dollars in potential savings and provides an unparalleled level of financial security.

Complimentary Maintenance Advantage

Furthermore, Hyundai includes Complimentary Maintenance for 3 years or 36,000 miles, covering normal, factory-scheduled services like oil changes and tire rotations. This further reduces the total cost of ownership and ensures your vehicle is maintained to the highest standards from day one.

When considering the complete ownership experience, the Kona's warranty and maintenance package creates a value proposition that is simply unmatched in the segment.
