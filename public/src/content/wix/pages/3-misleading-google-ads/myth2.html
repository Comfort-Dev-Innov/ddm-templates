Google strongly recommends you turn on Data Signals.

Sounds smart, in theory, right? More data is better. Google's recommendation must be the way to go. Except turning on Data Signals might be hiding up to 40% of your actual conversions.

I'm going to show you data from a car dealer running lead generation campaigns. Same time period (last 28 days). However, we set up two separate GA4 properties with different settings.

Property 1 - Data signals turned ON (Google's recommendation):
  • 778 total conversions
  • 6 visible conversion events
  • 412 total users
  • Orange warning icon: "Thresholding applied"
Property 2 - Data signals turned OFF:
  • 1,095 total conversions
  • 12 visible conversion events
  • 579 total users
  • Green checkmark: "Unsampled report - based on 100% of available data"
That's 317 more conversions (a 40% difference!) just from changing one setting. Same website, same traffic, and the same time period.

To make matters worse, the first property showed only 6 conversion events, while the second showed 12. Half of the conversion types weren't even visible when Data Signals was on.

Understanding the Problem

So what's going on here?

When you turn on Data Signals (which Google recommends during setup), you're allowing Google to blend your actual website data with modeled data from their broader user signals.

This triggers something called "thresholding." You'll see a little orange caution cone at the top of your reports with a message: "Google Analytics has applied thresholding to one or more cards in this report."

According to Google, thresholding is applied "to prevent anyone viewing a report from inferring the identity or sensitive information of individual users." When you have lower user counts in your date range and Data Signals is enabled, Google starts hiding data.

The data isn't gone; it happened. Real people converted on your website. However, Google won't show it to you.

So what do you do to fix it?

Here's what I recommend:

Create two GA4 properties under the same account:

  1. Property with Data Signals ON - Use this for building remarketing audiences (Google requires Data Signals for audience creation in Google Ads)
  2. Property with Data Signals OFF - Use this for actual reporting and analysis
