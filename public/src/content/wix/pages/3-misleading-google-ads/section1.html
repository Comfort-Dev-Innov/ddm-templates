How to Fix Misleading Google Ads Data

  • Segment by conversion action in Google Ads to see which conversions are actually changing
  • Ignore Store Visits data in your decision-making
  • Focus on verifiable conversion actions: forms, trackable calls, chat conversations
  • If Store Visits fluctuate wildly but other metrics are stable, trust the other metrics
  • Create two GA4 properties under your account
  • Turn Data Signals OFF on one property for clean reporting
  • Keep Data Signals ON on the other for remarketing audiences
  • Watch for the orange "thresholding applied" warning cone
  • Compare both properties regularly to understand what data you're missing
  • Implement third-party call tracking (We recommend CallRail)
  • Listen to actual calls to verify quality and count
  • Understand Google's definitions: "Phone Calls" = clicks/dials, "Call from Ads" = 30+ seconds
  • Report internally on actual conversations, not platform metrics
  • Cross-reference Google's numbers with your call tracking system
Another important thing to do is to always question everything and double-check everything.

Not everything that Google recommends as a default setting is good. You need to really explore outside the bounds to get accurate reporting.

Test these things for yourself.

If you have events and conversions set up properly in your account, try the dual GA4 property approach. Compare Store Visits to your actual foot traffic. Install call tracking and see how it aligns with Google's numbers.
