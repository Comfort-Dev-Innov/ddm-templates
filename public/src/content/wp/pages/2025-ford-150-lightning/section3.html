Electrifying Performance and Driving Dynamics

With its state-of-the-art electric powertrain, the 2025 Ford F-150 Lightning Base ushers in a new era of performance without compromise.

Powertrain and Acceleration

All Base models come equipped with a dual-motor all-wheel-drive system delivering 452 horsepower and a robust 775 lb-ft of torque. This torque figure remains consistent across all battery options, ensuring powerful and instant acceleration.

The electric motor configuration enables the Base variant to complete the 0 to 60 mph sprint in roughly 4.2 seconds, showcasing acceleration that surpasses many traditional gas-powered trucks. Drivers in areas such as Kent and Auburn will appreciate the smooth yet assertive power delivery offered in urban settings and highway merges along SR-167.

Range and Charging Capabilities

The 2025 F-150 Lightning Base utilizes a 98 kWh battery, rated for an EPA-estimated range of approximately 240 miles on a full charge. This is adequate for daily commuting and moderate hauling needs common in suburban areas like Maple Valley.

Charging is supported by a maximum DC fast charge rate of 150 kW, allowing the battery to recharge from 15% to 80% in as little as 32 to 39 minutes, depending on charger availability. Additionally, the included Mobile Power Cord features an adapter compatible with Tesla's NACS chargers—expanding charging options across the Puget Sound region.

The F-150 Lightning also incorporates real-time charging feedback, universal charge limit setting, and location-based schedule planning, optimizing energy management for unpredictable West Coast traffic patterns.

Towing and Payload Capacity

Strength remains a cornerstone, as the Base model can tow up to 7,700 pounds when equipped with the Standard Range battery. While this is below the top-tier configuration limits, it still supports the hauling needs for landscaping or recreational trailers typical around Kent's commercial districts.

Payload capabilities approach 2,000 pounds, albeit slightly constrained by battery mass, particularly compared to gas-powered counterparts. The Max Trailer Tow package, available on select trims, enhances towing with features such as an e-locking rear axle, trailer brake controller, and hill descent control—though not standard on the Base trim, these are potential upgrades at Bowen Scarff Ford.
