Fuel Economy and Efficiency in The 2026 Kona and 2026 Niro

Fuel economy considerations vary notably by drivetrain preference. The Kona, running solely on gasoline engines for 2026, balances economy and performance effectively for urban and highway driving in cities like Norman and Moore:

  • Base 2.0L FWD achieves EPA ratings of up to 29 mpg city and 35 mpg highway.
  • Turbo 1.6L FWD SUVs target up to 26 mpg city and 32 mpg highway.
  • The AWD versions weigh in slightly lower in mpg but compensate with enhanced traction capabilities.

Though the Niro's hybrid and plug-in hybrid variants boast exceptional EPA ratings, including city mileage that can exceed 50 mpg and plug-in electric ranges near 33 miles, these come with trade-offs such as a lack of AWD, higher curb weight, and significant compromises in cargo space. For most Oklahoma metro drivers seeking an all-around daily driver, the Kona's efficient gasoline engines combined with the AWD option and respectable fuel economy represent a compelling balance between performance and economy.
