Performance and Driving Experience of the 2026 Kona and 2026 Niro

Powertrain Options and Engine Output

The 2026 Hyundai Kona impresses with two gasoline engine options tuned for both capability and refinement:

  • A 2.0-liter naturally aspirated inline-4 producing 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft torque paired with a smooth Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT).
  • An invigorating 1.6-liter turbocharged inline-4 delivering 190 horsepower and 195 lb-ft torque, combined with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

Both powertrains offer optional all-wheel drive (AWD), ensuring better handling and traction for Oklahoma's varied road conditions, from snowfalls near Norman to rainy days in Edmond.

The Kia Niro, while primarily hybrid-oriented, offers:

  • A hybrid and plug-in hybrid drivetrain powered by a 1.6-liter four-cylinder paired with electric motors, producing 139 horsepower for the hybrid and 180 horsepower for the PHEV model.

Notably, no AWD option is available for the Niro, restricting its adaptability on slippery or rugged terrain.


Acceleration and Handling

The Kona's turbo 1.6L AWD earns credible respect with a brisk 0-60 mph time of approximately 7.5 seconds, faster than the Niro PHEV's roughly 7.2 seconds, delivering sporty responsiveness that complements everyday commuting with confident handling.


The Niro's hybrid and plug-in variants, while excellent for fuel savings, lack the Kona's all-wheel-drive option and spirited driving feel. The Kona's suspension system upgrades, independent front MacPherson struts coupled with an available multi-link rear on AWD models, offer precision steering and composed cornering unmatched by the Niro's more economy-focused tuning.


Braking and Maneuverability

Braking systems are robust in both models; however, Kona benefits from its electronically assisted parking brake and motor-driven power steering that contribute to a tighter turning radius (curb-to-curb: 34.8 feet), which improves agility in urban traffic, a plus for drivers in Oklahoma City and nearby communities.

Trim-by-Trim Overview of the 2026 Hyundai Kona and 2026 Kia Niro

The 2026 Hyundai Kona lineup offers four distinct trims (SE, SEL Sport, SEL Premium, and Limited), giving drivers a clear progression from practicality to premium comfort.


Kona SE: The entry-level SE provides excellent value with a 12.3-inch touchscreen display, wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, and Hyundai SmartSense safety features, including forward collision avoidance and lane-keeping assist.


Kona SEL Premium: Designed for drivers who prioritize comfort and technology, the SEL Premium introduces a power driver's seat, heated front seats, dual-zone automatic climate control, and a Bose premium audio system. It also comes in the SEL Sport, which adds sportier styling, upgraded alloy wheels, and additional comfort features, giving the Kona a more dynamic personality without a major price jump.


Kona Limited: Sitting at the top of the lineup, the Limited includes upscale details like leather-trimmed seats, a surround-view monitor, blind-spot view camera, Hyundai Digital Key 2 Touch, and a hands-free smart liftgate. Its refined cabin and advanced tech make it feel a class above its size.


Every trim offers confident performance, with the 2.0L engine standard and the turbocharged 1.6L available for those seeking extra power. All-wheel drive can also be added for better control on Oklahoma's unpredictable roads.


The 2026 Kia Niro, meanwhile, organizes its lineup around efficiency-focused options rather than performance or drivetrain flexibility. The Hybrid is expected in LX, EX, EX Touring, SX, and SX Touring trims, while the Plug-In Hybrid will likely offer EX and SX Touring models.


Niro LX: Covers the basics, focusing on efficiency with hybrid power and essential driver assistance.


Niro EX / EX Touring: Adds tech and comfort features, including a larger infotainment system and more refined interior materials.


Niro SX / SX Touring: Brings premium touches such as ventilated front seats, upgraded audio, and a digital instrument display.


While the Niro's higher trims improve comfort and connectivity, all versions remain front-wheel drive only, emphasizing fuel savings over versatility.
