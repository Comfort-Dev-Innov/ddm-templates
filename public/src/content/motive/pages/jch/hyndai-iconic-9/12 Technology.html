Technology, Connectivity, and Safety


Hyundai has built its reputation on offering a high level of standard safety and technology, and both the IONIQ 9 and Santa Cruz benefit from this philosophy. While both vehicles receive a comprehensive suite of features, the IONIQ 9 serves as the showcase for Hyundai's next- generation systems, giving buyers a clear choice based on how much they value being on the absolute cutting edge.


Both models offer large, user-friendly touchscreens with wireless Apple CarPlay® and Android Auto™ integration. The IONIQ 9, however, standardizes a stunning panoramic curved display that seamlessly integrates a 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster and a 12.3-inch infotainment screen. The Santa Cruz features a standard 12.3-inch touchscreen, with the dual-screen setup reserved for higher trims. The IONIQ 9 also boasts more advanced convenience technologies, including the available Digital Key 2 (allowing your smartphone to act as a key), Remote Smart Parking Assist 2, and a full-color Head-Up Display.


On the safety front, both vehicles come standard with the robust Hyundai SmartSense suite, which includes essentials like Forward Collision- Avoidance Assist (FCA), Lane Keeping Assist (LKA), and Blind-Spot Collision-Avoidance Assist (BCA). This establishes a high safety floor across the board. The IONIQ 9, as the flagship, receives the most advanced versions of these systems, such as FCA 2, which offers more precision and capability. For Oklahoma drivers, features like Highway Driving Assist—available on both models—make the I-35 commute between Oklahoma City and Norman significantly safer and less stressful.

Practicality and Utility for Oklahoma Life


This is where the core missions of the IONIQ 9 and Santa Cruz diverge most clearly, revealing their unique strengths. The IONIQ 9's utility is about providing immense, versatile, and secure interior space. Santa Cruz's utility is about offering the ultimate flexibility for messy, bulky, or tall items that have no place inside a cabin.


The IONIQ 9 functions as a massive, configurable container. With its third- and second-row seats folded, it opens up a cavernous 86.9 cubic feet of enclosed cargo space, perfect for securely hauling everything from luggage for a family vacation to valuable equipment for a small business. It also features a small front trunk, or "frunk," for additional storage. A groundbreaking feature is its bidirectional charging capability, known as Vehicle-to-Load (V2L). This allows the IONIQ 9 to function as a mobile power source, a feature that could be invaluable for powering essential appliances during one of Oklahoma's notorious power outages.


The Santa Cruz, meanwhile, is an open platform for adventure and work. Its 4-foot composite bed offers 27 cubic feet of volume, ideal for items that are too dirty or bulky for an SUV's interior. It is perfectly suited for hauling bags of mulch for a garden in Bethany, tossing muddy sports gear in the back after a game at a local park, or carrying tools for a DIY project. The bed's utility is enhanced by a lockable under-floor storage compartment and an available integrated tonneau cover that provides weather-resistant, secure storage. Critically, with the available turbocharged engine and AWD, the Santa Cruz matches the IONIQ 9's maximum towing capacity of 5,000 pounds, forcing a decision based on the type of utility needed most often, not just raw numbers.

Value, Warranty, and Long-Term Ownership


The financial aspect of purchasing a new vehicle extends far beyond the initial sticker price. It involves considering the total cost of ownership, including fuel, maintenance, and the peace of mind that comes with a great warranty. The IONIQ 9 commands a premium starting MSRP, from approximately $58,955 to $76,490, reflecting its status as a large, high-tech electric vehicle. However, this higher upfront investment is offset by significantly lower long-term operating costs. Electricity is substantially cheaper than gasoline, and with no engine oil, spark plugs, or complex transmission fluid to change, routine maintenance is simpler and less expensive.


The Santa Cruz offers a more accessible entry point, with MSRPs ranging from approximately $29,500 to $43,450. This makes it an excellent value proposition for buyers seeking utility and style. While its long-term costs will include gasoline and traditional engine maintenance, it remains a cost-effective choice in the compact truck segment.


Regardless of the model chosen, every new Hyundai is backed by America's Best Warranty. This includes a 10-year/100,000-mile powertrain limited warranty and a 5-year/60,000-mile new vehicle limited warranty, providing unparalleled peace of mind for owners across the Oklahoma City metro region.



The Right Hyundai for You


The 2026 Hyundai IONIQ 9 stands out as the superior choice for those pursuing the future of automotive technology. It is perfectly suited for the growing family in Edmond or Norman who prioritizes premium space, cutting-edge safety, and the quiet, powerful performance of an electric powertrain. If your daily life involves carpooling, family road trips, and a desire to eliminate trips to the gas station without compromising on luxury or capability, the IONIQ 9 is the vehicle for you.


The 2026 Hyundai Santa Cruz is the best solution for those who live an active, hands-on lifestyle. It is the ideal partner for the resident of Yukon or Moore who frequently tackles DIY projects, needs the flexibility of an open bed for sports or outdoor gear, and wants a comfortable, capable vehicle that is easy to drive and park anywhere in the OKC metro. If your weekends are spent exploring, creating, and doing, Santa Cruz offers a unique blend of utility and comfort that no traditional SUV or truck can match.
