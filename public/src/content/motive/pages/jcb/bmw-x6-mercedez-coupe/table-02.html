Underpinning the BMW X6's performance is an advanced lineup designed to satisfy both spirited drivers from Choctaw and families from Piedmont seeking versatility.

  • Base xDrive40i Model: Powered by a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six with mild-hybrid technology, it delivers a robust 375 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, enabling a brisk 0-60 mph in just around 5.2 seconds. This balance of power and efficiency is fine-tuned for urban commutes as well as highway cruising around Mustang or Bethany.
  • M60i xDrive: Elevating the performance bar, the 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 pumps out 523 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque, pushing the sprint to 60 mph in an estimated 4.2 seconds paired with a refined M Sport exhaust and braking system.
  • M Competition Variant: Demanding ultimate performance? The M Competition answers with 617 horsepower, yielding exhilarating 3.5-second acceleration. This power is expertly managed by a reinforced chassis and a highly capable adaptive suspension setup, and extends the X6's sports car DNA while maintaining SUV comfort.

The BMW xDrive all-wheel drive system ensures traction and composure on local Edmond roads, while adaptive suspensions tailor ride dynamics between comfort and athleticism. In comparison, the standard engine for the 2026 Mercedes-Benz GLE Coupe (GLE 450 4MATIC) is a 3.0-liter inline-6 turbo, producing 375 hp and 369 lb-ft of torque, integrated with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system. The drivetrain utilizes a 9-speed automatic transmission (9G-TRONIC) and the 4MATIC permanent all-wheel-drive system for optimal traction and performance.
