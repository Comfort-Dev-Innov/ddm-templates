Driving Dynamics of the 2026 Santa Cruz and The 2026 Frontier

The differences in drivetrain technology and off-road hardware further define the distinct roles of the Santa Cruz and Frontier.

The Santa Cruz offers standard Front-Wheel Drive (FWD) on its entry-level trims, with Hyundai's advanced HTRAC All-Wheel Drive (AWD) system available as an option or as standard equipment on higher trims. HTRAC is an intelligent, proactive system that constantly monitors driving conditions and automatically distributes power between the front and rear axles to ensure optimal traction. It's a "set it and forget it" system, perfect for providing confidence on rain-slicked roads, in wintry conditions, or on gravel paths without requiring any driver intervention. The Santa Cruz XRT trim is geared toward adventure, featuring standard HTRAC AWD with an enhanced Terrain Mode Select, unique 18-inch wheels, and rugged styling cues. It is perfectly suited for navigating dirt roads to a campsite or trailhead.

The Frontier adheres to a more traditional truck setup, offering Rear-Wheel Drive (RWD) or a robust, part-time 4-Wheel Drive (4WD) system with a 2-speed transfer case for high- and low-range gearing. This is a mechanically direct system that the driver must manually engage, making it ideal for the deliberate demands of serious off-roading. The Frontier's commitment to off-road prowess is fully realized in the PRO-4X trim. This is not merely an appearance package; it is a dedicated off-road machine equipped with specialized Bilstein shocks, protective skid plates, all-terrain tires, and a crucial electronic locking rear differential. This hardware gives it the capability to tackle truly challenging terrain, such as the rugged trails found in the Ouachita National Forest. The gap in off-road intent is vast and deliberate. The Santa Cruz XRT is for the owner whose adventures are part of an active lifestyle like kayaking or hiking, and who needs a vehicle that can confidently get them and their gear to the starting point. The Frontier PRO-4X is built for the enthusiast who views conquering difficult trails as the primary mission. Both philosophies are valid, but Oklahoma buyers should recognize which adventure profile best describes their actual needs rather than aspirational ones.
