The 2025 Tucson Powertrain Lineup

Hyundai provides a clear "good, better, best" hierarchy with its powertrain offerings, ensuring there is a perfect match for every driver's priorities and budget.

  • 2.5L GDI/MPI 4-Cylinder (Gas): This is the standard engine across the gasoline-powered Tucson lineup. It produces a capable 187 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque, managed by a smooth 8-speed automatic transmission. Buyers can choose between standard Front-Wheel Drive (FWD) for maximum efficiency or opt for Hyundai's HTRAC All-Wheel Drive (AWD) for enhanced traction in Oklahoma's unpredictable weather. This powertrain achieves an EPA-estimated fuel economy of up to 25 MPG in the city and 33 MPG on the highway with FWD.

  • 1.6L Turbo Hybrid (HEV): Representing a significant leap in both performance and efficiency, the Tucson Hybrid combines a potent 1.6L turbocharged engine with an electric motor. This advanced system delivers a total output of 231 horsepower and a very strong 271 lb-ft of torque. Unlike some competitors that use a continuously variable transmission (CVT), the Tucson Hybrid employs a responsive 6-speed automatic, providing a more traditional and engaging driving feel. HTRAC AWD comes standard on all hybrid models. The efficiency-focused Blue trim leads the pack with an outstanding EPA estimate of 38 MPG in both city and highway driving.

  • 1.6L Turbo Plug-in Hybrid (PHEV): For those seeking the ultimate combination of power and electric capability, the Tucson Plug-in Hybrid (PHEV) stands at the top of the range. It boosts the total system output to 268 horsepower while maintaining the same robust 271 lb-ft of torque as the hybrid. Like the hybrid, it features a 6-speed automatic transmission and standard HTRAC AWD. The PHEV's key advantage is its 13.8 kWh battery, which allows for up to 32 miles of all-electric driving on a full charge. This means many local errands and commutes can be completed without using a drop of gasoline. When operating as a hybrid, it achieves a combined 35 MPG.
