Even with the set pricing structure of CPO vehicles, there are ways to maximize your value.

Timing Your Purchase

  • After New Models Arrive: When the brand-new model year Hyundai (e.g., the 2026 Palisade) arrives, dealerships often see an influx of trade-ins of the previous model (e.g., the 2023 or 2024 Palisade). This can lead to a great selection of late-model, low-mileage CPO vehicles.
  • Holiday Sales Events: Keep an eye out for special promotions during holiday weekends like Memorial Day, Labor Day, and year-end sales events, which may include special CPO financing rates.

Negotiating Your Deal

Yes, you can often negotiate on a CPO vehicle. The price reflects a great deal of built-in value, but a polite, informed negotiation is always welcome.

  • Focus on the "Out-the-Door" Price: This is the total cost, including the vehicle price, taxes, and all fees. It's the most important number.
  • Know Your Trade-In's Value: Use our online trade valuation tool before you visit. It takes seconds. Having a clear, independent value for your current car separates the trade-in negotiation from the vehicle purchase, simplifying the process for you.
  • Discuss Financing in Advance: While Hyundai often has excellent CPO rates, it can be helpful to get a pre-approval from your own bank or credit union. This gives you a benchmark and helps you recognize a great financing offer when you see one.
  • Be Prepared and Respectful: The most successful negotiations are collaborative conversations. By showing you've done your research and are serious about buying, our team can work with you to find a final price and payment that feels great.
