The 2026 Kona and Crosstrek's Power, Engine, and Drivetrain


A vehicle's character is defined by its powertrain. It dictates how it feels merging onto I-240 during rush hour, how efficiently it handles a commute to Norman, and how confidently it performs on a rain-slicked highway. Here, the Kona and Crosstrek showcase their fundamentally different approaches to performance.

2026 Hyundai Kona
2026 Subaru Crosstrek

The Kona

Hyundai's engineering philosophy for the 2026 Kona centers on personalization. It recognizes that Oklahoma drivers have diverse needs and offers two distinct gasoline engine options to match.

The Smart, Efficient Standard: The SE and SEL Sport trims come equipped with a 2.0-liter MPI-Atkinson 4-cylinder engine producing 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque. Paired with a smooth Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT), this powertrain is optimized for efficiency, making it the ideal choice for the daily commuter or the budget-conscious family seeking maximum miles per gallon on city streets and highways alike.


The Spirited Turbo Upgrade: For those who crave a more dynamic driving experience, the SEL Premium and Limited trims feature a potent 1.6-liter Turbo-GDI 4-cylinder engine. This powerhouse generates an impressive 190 horsepower and a robust 195 lb-ft of torque, available from a low 1,700 RPM. Mated to a quick-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission, this option delivers brisk acceleration for effortless highway merging and a truly engaging feel behind the wheel.


This dual-engine strategy is complemented by a choice of drivetrains. The standard Front-Wheel Drive (FWD) configuration maximizes fuel economy, a sensible advantage for the majority of Oklahoma's driving conditions. For those desiring extra traction and confidence during our state's occasional ice storms or heavy downpours, a capable All-Wheel Drive (AWD) system is available on every trim level. This allows buyers to pay only for the capability they truly need.


The Crosstrek

Subaru takes a more uniform approach with the 2026 Crosstrek. For the 2026 model year, all gasoline-powered Crosstrek trims now feature a 2.5-liter SUBARU BOXER速 4-cylinder engine as standard, producing approximately 180-182 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque. This is a welcome update from previous years, where a less powerful engine was standard on lower trims.


For those focused on efficiency, Subaru introduces a new 2.5-liter series-parallel hybrid system for 2026, delivering a combined output of 194 horsepower. Critically, every Crosstrek, regardless of trim or powertrain, comes standard with Subaru's renowned Symmetrical All-Wheel Drive. While this system is undeniably capable, it represents a mandated choice. Every Crosstrek buyer accepts the year-round mechanical drag and associated fuel economy penalty of a full-time AWD system, a capability that many drivers may only need a few days out of the year.


Real-World Performance

When comparing these philosophies, the Kona's flexibility emerges as a clear advantage for the Oklahoma driver. The ability to choose between an efficient FWD setup for most of the year or a secure AWD system for winter provides a more tailored and cost-effective solution. Furthermore, the Kona's available turbo engine delivers its peak torque much lower in the rev range (1,700 RPM) than the Crosstrek's gasoline engine, which translates to a more immediate and satisfying response in stop-and-go city traffic, a tangible benefit for anyone navigating the City. The Kona empowers the buyer with choice, while the Crosstrek presents a single, adventure-focused solution for all.
