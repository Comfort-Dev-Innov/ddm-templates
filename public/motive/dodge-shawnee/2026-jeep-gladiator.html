Introducing the 2026 Jeep Gladiator Base

The 2026 Jeep Gladiator Base caters to drivers around Shawnee, OK, and neighboring communities like Choctaw and Seminole who need a midsize pickup capable of handling both work demands and weekend adventures. Carrying five passengers comfortably, it pairs the iconic Jeep off-road prowess with the practicality of a five-foot pickup bed. The four-door extended wheelbase offers roominess unmatched in many trucks of its size, with front and rear passenger volumes spanning from 54 to 57 cubic feet up front and 50 to 53 cubic feet in the rear.

Under the hood, this Gladiator is powered by a 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine, producing 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant, coupled with a smooth 8-speed automatic transmission, propels the truck with confidence whether cruising urban streets in Moore or tackling rugged trails near McLoud. Four-wheel drive comes standard, ensuring traction on Oklahoma’s varied terrain.

For those in Newalla or Jones, its capability is proven in towing and hauling: the Gladiator Base can tow up to 7,700 pounds when equipped with the proper axle and tow packages, while maximum payload climbs to 1,725 pounds depending on configuration—ideal for both worksite tools and recreational gear.

Robust Performance and Driving Dynamics

The heart of the 2026 Gladiator Base is its reliable 3.6-liter V6 engine, designed with an aluminum block and four valves per cylinder to maximize power and efficiency. Located upfront, the engine’s 285 horsepower peaks at 6,400 rpm, while 260 lb-ft of torque becomes available at 4,400 rpm, delivering the performance necessary for daily drives and demanding excursions alike.

Paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, the truck offers smooth gear transitions optimized for fuel economy and responsiveness. In areas like Oklahoma City and Norman, the Gladiator feels robust yet manageable. Still, potential drivers should anticipate a firmer ride over uneven surfaces—a tradeoff for the off-road-ready suspension and solid frame.

Steering has been described as slightly imprecise, yet it aligns with the Gladiator’s rugged intent rather than a sports sedan’s nimbleness. Its acceleration from 0 to 60 mph clocks around 7.2 seconds, matching typical midsize pickup capabilities, with a top speed of roughly 97 mph.

Off-road seekers from surrounding towns like McLoud and Newalla will appreciate the Gladiator’s strengthened iron steering knuckles and reinforced frame. When equipped with optional packages, the truck gains sway bar disconnect technology and heavy-duty engine cooling, enhancing control and durability. Its trail performance is further supported by impressive ground clearance and specialized tires available on select trims.

Trim Choices and Customization Packages

While the Base trim of the Gladiator is a capable entry point for buyers in Choctaw, Shawnee, and surrounding communities, Jeep offers several packages to enhance capability and comfort:

  • Active Safety Group: Adds key driver aids, including blind-spot monitoring, rear parking sensors, and automatic high beams, ideal for safer daily driving in and around Moore or Norman.

  • Convenience Group: Takes comfort a step further with features such as heated front seats, remote start, a Gorilla Glass windshield for enhanced durability, and a digital instrument cluster.

  • Max Tow Package: Optimizes towing ability with a 4.10 axle ratio, all-terrain tires, an anti-spin rear differential, and reinforced engine cooling systems.

Each package is designed to tailor the Gladiator to individual needs, whether for active off-roading or enhanced commuting comfort.
